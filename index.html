Meet Mr. Sundar Pichai

CEO of Google

Sundar Pichai

Sundar Pichai is a prominent figure in the tech industry, known for his leadership at Google and its parent company, Alphabet Inc. Born in Chennai, India, in 1972, Pichai rose through the ranks with his exceptional engineering and managerial skills. He joined Google in 2004, where he played a pivotal role in the development of popular products like Chrome and Chrome OS. His dedication and vision led him to become the CEO of Google in 2015, and later, the CEO of Alphabet Inc. in 2019. Under his leadership, Google continued to innovate in areas such as artificial intelligence, cloud computing, and mobile technology. PRODUCTS

Google Chrome

Sundar Pichai joined Google in 2004 and played a pivotal role in the development of the company's core products. He contributed to the creation and improvement of Google Chrome, which has become one of the most popular web browsers globally.

Android

Pichai was involved in the development of Android, the world's most popular mobile operating system. His leadership helped Android grow and become the OS of choice for billions of smartphones and other devices.

Google Drive

Pichai was also involved in the development of Google Drive, the cloud storage service, which have contributed to Google's presence in cloud computing and education markets.

Google Assistant

Pichai played a role in the integration of artificial intelligence and machine learning into Google products, including Google Assistant. Google Assistant is a virtual assistant that provides voice-activated assistance and information.

It's important to note that Sundar Pichai was responsible for leading teams and making strategic decisions related to these products rather than being the sole creator. His leadership and vision were instrumental in the success of these products within Google. Additionally, after becoming the CEO of Alphabet Inc. in 2019, Pichai's responsibilities expanded beyond Google to oversee the broader portfolio of companies under Alphabet's umbrella, including subsidiaries like Waymo and Verily.
